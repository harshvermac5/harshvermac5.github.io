Over the past week here in Delhi, much of my time has been consumed by filling out countless job applications. Despite the effort, I'm yet to see any results. The job search can indeed be quite frustrating, especially when transitioning from finance to IT, as I have chosen to do, with a focus on Routing and Switching as inspired by figures like Sir Jeremy McDowell and Chuck Keith. However, it's important to note that certain factors vary depending on one's location.

Yesterday, I attended a Scalar webinar, where Google and Microsoft employees shared their insights on entering the realms of AI and Machine Learning.
The discussion gradually shifted to the current market dynamics and demands in India. I was surprised to know that AI and Machine Learning have roots dating back to the 90s, it wasn't until around 2015 that computers became powerful enough to effectively execute these algorithms at scale. At its core, AI and Machine Learning often boil down to a series of if-else statements trained over robust algorithm to draw meaningful conclusions.

From a financial perspective, not every company has the resources to maintain these models internally. Many opt to leverage APIs for a fee instead. Regarding job markets, while there is a growing demand for Machine Learning Engineers, the ratio suggests that only about one in ten companies require them.
This parallels the situation with Cybersecurity analysts, where the buzz around certain skills often outweighs the actual demand in the job market. This is because software engineers often acquire foundational knowledge in these areas themselves.

In conclusion, the job market still heavily favors Backend Engineers who are adept at upskilling themselves and utilizing AI to enhance their abilities. As the host also told, even mid to large-size companies like Scalar lack specialized security branches. So, what's the next step? Upskill yourself. Recognizing my own need to adopt an engineer's mindset and build upon available resources, I've embarked on learning Backend development, starting with Javascript. For this, I've turned to Shreiyans Coding School, a comprehensive YouTube channel that focuses on teaching the essentials in a memorable manner. As soon as this blog is posted, I'll be diving back into their playlist.

Thank you for joining me on this journey, and I look forward to sharing more with you next week.
