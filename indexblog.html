Hello and welcome! I'm glad you're here. This is my blog, where I showcase my IT skills and projects. Every week, I update you on my progress and challenges. Join me on my tech odyssey!

As you may know, I'm preparing for the CCNA examination, which is a certification that validates my knowledge and skills in network engineering and security. It's a tough exam, but I'm determined to ace it.

One of the ways I'm studying for the exam is by creating YouTube videos, mainly audio form recordings, where I explain the concepts and topics covered in the CCNA syllabus. I have a passion for sharing educational content with you all, and I believe that teaching is the best way to learn.

I was inspired by Puneet Biseria who is a great teacher and a mentor for me. He once said that you only grasp a hold of something if you are able to teach others. That resonated with me, and I decided to follow his advice.

I have a target of creating a set of 12 videos, covering the main domains of the CCNA exam. You can find them on my YouTube playlist for CCNA. I'm currently on the fourth video, where I talk about network access and security.

I know that the audio quality is not very good on the first few videos, but I hope you can bear with me. I'm still learning how to use the software and the microphone, and I'm trying to improve with each video. I appreciate your feedback and suggestions on how to make my videos better.

I'm also aware that it was the end of the year when I started this project, and everyone was celebrating the new year. But I was so focused on my goal that I didn't mind spending hours on my computer, recording and editing my videos. I had a mixed feeling of happiness and tension, as I was excited about sharing my knowledge with you, but also nervous about the upcoming exam.

I did make a special video, where I greeted you and wished you a happy new year. You can watch it here. I hope you enjoyed it and felt my gratitude and appreciation for your support and encouragement.

That's all for today. Thank you for reading my blog and watching my videos. I hope you learned something new and useful from them. I would love to hear from you! Your thoughts and feedback help me grow and improve my blog and my videos. Please share your comments below.

See you next week, when I'll update you on my fifth video and my exam preparation. Until then, stay safe and keep learning!
